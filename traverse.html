Traverse

Tools: Figma, Adobe After Effects, Interaction Flow, Market Research

Project Overview

Traverse is a rebrand and redesign of the Septa app – a mobile app that helps public transportation users in the Philadelphia area get from one destination to another. It is a utility app that provides information on different methods of transportation and their specific lines, locations, stops, schedules, and arrival times. For my User Interaction class, we focused on the importance of microinteractions such as animations and feedback in apps, so the final product created is an After Effects animation of a task flow within Traverse.

The Problem

In App Store reviews, many users expressed dissatisfaction with the confusing and outdated interface of the app and the inability to search for routes by nearest location, as the app only allows users to manually input both start and destination stations. While the app shows alerts, it lacks important features such as real-time updates on train and bus schedules (which Septa only offers through a separate service called ReadyPhiladelphia, which some users might not have or be aware of). This may result in frustrated users that miss their intended departure times or experience unexpected delays.

The Solution

The redesign prioritizes usability and simplicity, with a clear and intuitive interface that allows users to easily plan their trips and get real-time updates on train and bus schedules. The app will allow users to search for not just stations, but places they want to go to and suggest stations near their destination as well as the distances of the station. The alerts tab has been moved from the bottom navigation to an alert icon in the top left that provides real time alerts on recently viewed trips. These changes, along with a cleaner interface, will hopefully help users find directions and schedules easier and keep them updated and informed, hopefully resulting in a more sustainable and efficient transit system.

Project Goal

The app’s goal is to provide a simple, intuitive interface that allows users to quickly search for routes and schedules in order to easily navigate Philly. Features of this app include input of desired locations and method of transportation, searching for a nearby stop, and viewing maps and schedules.

Design

User Persona

There isn’t a set demographic for people that use public transportation, as public transit is for everyone, regardless of race, age, or sometimes demographic. The majority of public transit users live in the city where it is hard to drive and more convenient to catch a bus or train.

While there is not just one user to design for, the users that would use the app the most would be the ones that commute regularly on public transportation and take different routes to different locations, thus needing the app to help them navigate unfamiliar routes on the daily.

Styles

Design wise, I wanted to keep certain elements of the original SEPTA app, but create a cleaner and more minimalistic feel, similar to websites like Apple. Because this app would be used by a wide variety of users, it must be designed to be accessible and intuitive to all.

Microinteractions

I mapped sketched out microinteraction ideas for two loading screens. Some other microactions I will include in the app are tabs that slide up and fade in/out transitions. After sketching and brainstorming, I created wireframes to help bring the app to life.

User Flow

In the beginning of the design process, I mapped out the user task of viewing the schedule of a specific train line on the original SEPTA app and created an IX flowchart for Traverse based on that, with a more intuitive flow. In the chart, I defined each interactive element’s trigger, feedback, rules, and loops/modes
